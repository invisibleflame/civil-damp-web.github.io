Minors

Minor is an additional credential a student will earn if he/she does minimum 30 credits worth of additional learning in a discipline other than his/her major discipline of B.Tech/DD/Int. M.Sc. degree.

Computer Science (CS) Minor

CS Minor


1

Review 1

By Prajwal.K.A

A total of 30 credits must be completed (5 courses of 6 credits each is the usual pattern).

The importance of computer science in any field is known to all. In recent times, basic knowledge of computer science has become an integral part of Civil engineering. In some abroad universities, some of the computer science courses are offered by the Civil engineering department itself for its students.

Many complex problems of civil engineering which cannot be solved manually are solved with the help of computers. It is easier to model a structure, simulate the conditions and test its stability rather than manually solving for its stability. For using such softwares, this minor is not required. But for building such softwares, this minor helps a lot. This minor will help you write better programs which give accurate results in lesser amount of time.

The students who want to pursue a career in non-core fields like coding, finance etc will also find great use of the things learnt in this minor.

The usual courses available are:
  • CS 213: Data Structures and Algorithms
  • CS 218: Design and Analysis of Algorithms
  • CS 224: Computer Networks
  • CS 347: Operating Systems
  • CS 408: Graph Theory
  • CS 416: Computer and Network Security
These courses have also been offered sometimes:
  • CS 228: Logic for Programming
  • CS 317: Databases and Information Systems, etc
Usually 3 minor courses run each semester in the 5th slot (Wed, Fri – 9:30-11:00 AM). Any other course apart from these can also be taken up and tagged as a minor course with the consent of the instructor.
Some courses like Design and Analysis of Algorithms and Operating systems have Data Structures and Algorithms as their pre-requisites. Most of the other usual courses don’t have any. But basic knowledge of coding is required (CS101).

For a person who loves challenges, this minor has a lot to offer. All the courses are really interesting, but at the same time difficult also. The effort that one has to put in to score decently is much more when compared to the normal courses in civil engineering. Usually the course contents are vast. Some of these courses are coupled with a lab course for the mainstream CS students but minor students can’t afford the luxury of having a lab also in a 6 credit course. So, it makes the understanding of certain concepts even more difficult. But the professors also usually put in a lot of effort in teaching and helping the students understand the concepts.

The CPI cut off for most of the courses is around 8.5-8.7. But some professors take in extra students if the student approaches him/her and requests. So, usually the competition is very high and it requires a lot of effort to score a good grade (given that the grading in some of the courses is also tough).

It varies from course to course. Most courses follow the usual pattern of quizzes, midsem and endsem. You should expect assignments or projects or extra quizzes in most of the courses. Plagiarism has serious consequences. Some exams are open notes exams (you can carry hand-written materials). The examinations are usually difficult. Direct questions from class notes or example problems are rare sights. One should apply their logic and understanding of the concept in each question. Even some of the assignments are very challenging. As a result, you will understand the subject better.
Difficult
Reiterating, be regular if you can and make sure to carry the course booklet and a calculator (He really does send people out without exception). Being the first class conducted in the department gives a nice feel to it, with the entire section sitting together and all. On the whole it was one of the more fun courses of that semester, so as long as you don’t take it too lightly you should be fine.

CESE Minor


1

Review 1

By Shraddha Rana

30 credits – 5 courses of 6 credits each.
Environmental Science is a sister branch of Civil Engineering. I took up this minor because of the course content. There were a lot of overlapping topics with the Civil Engineering curriculum. It seemed interesting and useful to know what impacts human activities have on the environment and how they can be rectified. As a Civil engineer it is our duty to use sustainable practices, whenever we design or build something, which don’t harm the environment. So I felt doing a minor in Environmental Science will help me achieve the same.
  • ES 203: Water and Wastewater Engineering
  • ES 303: Municipal Solid and Biomedical Waste Management
  • ES 401: Environmental Management
  • ES 204: Environmental Chemistry
  • ES 306: Environmental Systems Modelling
  • ES 444 Or ES 658: Environmental Change and Sustainable Development
There are no course pre-requisites for the list above. And generally there are no CPI cut-offs either. It’s very easy to get courses in this minor. Except for the ES306 course, they have only 5 seats for minor students so it’s advisable to fill it up asap.
Knowledge of Environmental Science is important to all engineers. We should make sure that our designs ,products and their corresponding processes should have the least impact on the environment. This minor gives a taste towards the same goal. ES204 gives a basic view on the chemistry behind environmental process, both natural and ones caused by man. ES306 is very useful as modelling is used in almost all spheres of work. ES401 has lessons on laws and regulations as well. If you wish to work in industries this will be useful. ES203 ans ES303 help in knowing about waste management. And ES658 teaches about sustainable practices. All these courses together give a sense of how we can reduce negative impact on the environment which should be the our key self-responsibility as engineers.
The midsem and endsem pattern is quite like other courses, with a weightage of around 20-30% and 40-50% respectively. Most courses have assignments in place of quizzes which can be easily solved in your hostel. And almost all have a term paper of around 10% in which you present your study on a topic related to the course. Term papers should be prepared well in advance because the Professors attach a lot of importance to it. Most have a strict attendance policy too.
This minor is of medium difficulty. Regular completion of assignments helps in getting a good grade. But these same assignments and the above mentioned term paper take up quite a lot of time if you decide to do them properly. So one should be ready for the time commitment. In regards of the course content, it’s easy to follow if you attend the lectures and maintain class notes. There is less possibility of getting completely lost in these courses as compared to say some CS or EE minor courses. A lot of the course content may seem a repetition of few courses from the Civil Engineering curriculum, especially fluid mechanics, hydraulics and water resources. So that helps in understanding easily and further strengthening the concepts.

Energy Science & Engineering (EN) Minor

Energy Science Minor


1

Review 1

By Anushree Singh

30 credits necessary to complete the minor. A total of 5 courses need to be done out of which 4 are compulsory courses and 1 is an elective which can be chosen from the list of courses given below.

Energy is the new “hot topic”. In today’s world where everyone is shifting from older technologies to the newer ones, there are a lot of opportunities upcoming in this field. No matter what is happening in the world, Energy is one thing which will always be in demand and so will be the bright minds to contribute to it.

This minor program gives a good understanding about different dimensions of energy sciences including management, economics and renewable technologies etc. Although this minor is not quite related to the curriculum of civil engineering, however it definitely has its applications. Example- green building technologies, construction of power generation plants etc.

Following 4 courses are compulsory:
  • EN 403 Energy Resource, Environment and Economics
  • EN 301 Introduction to Renewable Energy Technologies
  • EN 302 Power Generation and Systems Planning
  • EN 402 Energy Management
One additional course can be chosen out of the following:
  • EN 613 Nuclear Reactor Theory
  • EN 615 Wind Energy Conversion Systems
  • EN 616 Direct Energy Conversion
  • EN 617 Thermodynamic Analysis of Industrial Systems
  • EN 619 Solar Energy for Industrial Process Heat
  • EN 624 Conversion of Energy in Buildings
  • EN 628 Materials and Devices for Energy Conversion
  • EN 630 Utilization of Solar Thermal Energy
  • EN 632 Waste to Energy
  • EN 634 Nuclear Reactor Thermal Hydraulics & Safety
  • EN 640 Solar photovoltaics: fundamentals, technology and applications
  • EN 645 Process integration
  • EN 646 Energy and climate
There are no pre requisites. CPI cut off can be 6.5-7.
There is a lot of variety in the kind of courses offered. However learning is restricted only to classrooms and no practical experience is offered. Some courses like Power Generation and Systems Planning have a lot of content based on thermodynamics and mathematics which will require an additional effort from a civil engineering student. This minor gives us a good insight into the global energy scenario and various technologies to make energy production and supply more advanced.
Generally all courses have Quiz 1 and 2, midsem and endsem. Additionally there is a course project which needs to be presented at the end of the course. However it is up to the professor to determine the exam pattern and nothing can be stated for sure.
The courses vary in difficulty from very east to very difficult. However the overall minor is towards the easy side. Most of the courses give 70% weightage to numericals and 30% to theory.

Electrical Engineering (EE) Minor

EE207 Electronic Devices and Circuits


1

Review 1

By Vinayak Suthar

Prof. Ashwin Tulapurkar
Main motive behind this course is to introduce students to basics of semiconductor fuctioning and devices.This course is the first step towards understanding of complex functioning of semiconductor devices. If you are curious about how electronic devices are developed and what’s the science. This course will help you develop an in-depth understanding of the physics behind electronic devices and will provide you the basic tools to go deeper into the subject
This course covers the basic concepts of :-
  • Crystal Properties ( Crystal Lattices, Periodic Structures, Cubic Lattices, Planes and Directions, Diamond Lattice)
  • Quantum Mechanics (Schrodinger Wave Equation, Potential Well Problem, Tunneling )
  • Energy Bands and Charge Carriers in semiconductors (Bonding forces in solids, Effective mass, Electrons and Holes in Quantum Wells, Carrier Concentration Dependencies, Carrier Drift in Electric and Magnetic fields , Hall effect)
  • Excess Carriers (Carrier Lifetime, Diffusion of Carriers, Recombination, Haynes-Shockley Experiment, Quasi-Fermi Levels)
  • Junctions ( Equilibrium conditions, Biased junctions, Steady State conditions, Transient conditions, Metal-Semiconductor junctions)
  • Field-Effect Transistors (Transistor operation, Junction FET, Metal Semiconductor FET, MOSFET)
NIL, Knowledge of EE 101 course would be helpful.
The pre-midsem portion is generally familiar to most of the students but it is important that you are aware and up to date to course. As slides are not provided, it is necessary to attend lectures for understanding and to perform well in course.Course content is vast and theoretical i.e. why some students loose interest in middle of the course.
Course evaluation is as follows:
Pre midsem Quiz – 15 marks
Midsem Exam – 30 marks
Post Midsem Quiz – 15 marks
Endsem Quiz- 40 marks
Generally Professors allow formulae sheet during the exam, questions in the exams are conceptual and easy.Attending tutorial sessions is very beneficial as mostly questions have a strong relevance to tutorials.
Moderate level difficulty
D. A. Neamen, Semiconductor Physics and Devices (IRWIN), Times Mirror High Education Group, Chicago)1997
E.S. Yang, Microelectronic Devices, McGraw Hill, Singapore, 1988
B.G. Streetman, Solid State Electronic Devices, Prentice Hall of India, New Delhi, 1995

Management Minor


1

Review 1

By Anonymous

Total credits-30. 5 courses which are 6 credits each.
If the student wants to explore what management has in store and considers a ‘non-core’ inclination, the minor provides a fair insight.
Marketing Management, Introduction to Accounts and Finance, International Competitiveness, Human Resource Management, Operations Management and Project Management are the 6 courses offered at present.
None of the courses have any prerequisites
The courses are very varied and provide a good introduction to management. Covering the major fields involved in management, it can appeal to the student’s interest and give him/her a good direction. Since the courses are independent of each other and have varied difficulty, they provide good information even as standalone courses. Completing the minor for a holistic view is recommended.
All courses have a different structure. Generally assignments (either in-class or home), a midsem and an endsem form the bulk of the pattern.
Most of the courses are intuitive and easy. Some students may find the Introduction to Accounts and Finance course difficult.
