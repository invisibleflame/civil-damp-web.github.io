Tips :
""For Mastercard, There was an aptitude test which is of same format as CAT exam and questions from CAT exams got repeated in our test.
You can go through the previous year’s CAT questions to improve your aptitude.
In interview I had three rounds.
First round was focused towards the guesstimates, simple coding question of reversing a string and some resume based questions. It was around 30 minutes
Second round was focused mainly towards my resume. They asked me about my projects, internships and another coding question of binary search. It was around 20 minutes.
Third round was the HR round where general HR questions were asked like:
Introduce yourself
What does company do?
Why do you want to join it?
How does Mastercard generate revenue?
Tell us about your strengths.
The Third round was conducted for about 15 mins.
The simple advice I would give is be thorough with your strengths and resume.
Do some aptitude prep and guesstimates.
It would be helpful if you can practice some basic Data structures like arrays and strings and basic searching and sorting algorithm. ""

Tips :
For those targeting BA profiles:

- Start with general prep of apti (quant, LR, DI are important, Verbal will only be there in few firms). I would strongly suggest to prepare just like you are appearing for CAT even if you do not plan to.
- Solving puzzles is THE most crucial part in preparation. They are asked not only in the test but constitute majority time of the interview as well.
- For cases solve the basic ones (guesstimates and profitability)
- Read a newspaper daily (financial preferred). You may not understand its benefit while in process but will definitely make you confident and well aware during the GDs and interview.
